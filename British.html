EYFS

In the British school system, there is no specific term "kindergarten." Instead, early education for children aged three to five is referred to as the Foundation Stage within the Early Years Foundation Stage (EYFS) framework. Here's a summary: Age Group: Foundation Stage is for children aged three to five, including the final year of nursery and the Reception year. Curriculum: The EYFS framework focuses on seven areas of learning and development, covering various aspects such as personal, social, and emotional development, literacy, mathematics, and more. Play-Based Learning: The emphasis is on play-based learning, allowing children to explore and develop skills through a combination of planned and child-initiated activities. Assessment: Ongoing observation and assessment are used to track each child's progress in different areas. This helps tailor support to individual needs. Transition: Foundation Stage serves as a transition to Key Stage 1, providing a solid foundation for future learning.

Elementary

The elementary stage in the British school system consists of Key Stage 1 (ages 5-7) and Key Stage 2 (ages 7-11). The curriculum includes core subjects such as English, Mathematics, and Science, with additional subjects introduced in Key Stage 2. The teaching approach focuses on foundational skills, and assessments, including SATs, may be conducted in Year 2 and Year 6. The transition to secondary school occurs at the end of Year 6. The curriculum aims to provide a well-rounded education, emphasizing academic, physical, and social development. Extracurricular activities and a holistic approach contribute to a comprehensive educational experience.

Middle and High school

Primary Education: Key Stages 1 and 2 cover ages 5 to 11. Subjects include English, mathematics, science, and more. Secondary Education: Key Stage 3 spans ages 11 to 14 (Years 7 to 9). Key Stage 4 covers ages 14 to 16 (Years 10 and 11) and leads to GCSE exams. Students specialize in subjects, including core subjects and electives. Post-16 Education: After Key Stage 4, students may pursue post-16 education, studying A-levels or vocational qualifications. The educational system may vary across the UK, and individual schools may have unique features. For specific details, refer to regional education authorities or school policies.
